Hey — I'm John Reed. 28 years in tech and still hooked. I started out racking
servers as a SysAdmin and grew into cloud architecture and site reliability —
years of it spent keeping distributed databases and 99.99% systems honest
(Galera, sharded MariaDB, the kind of replication that has to heal itself).
Hence the name of this place: *eventually consistent* is both what those systems
promise and how I've come to think good engineering — and most opinions — settle.

Along the way: time inside AWS as a TAM, cloud-architecture and FinOps work that
clawed back seven figures in spend, and more compliance-driven Terraform than I
can count. These days I'm all-in on AI as a force multiplier for the way I build —
on real infrastructure, not demos.

Right now I'm heads-down on the fun stuff: blockchain, zero-knowledge
cryptography, compliance-driven Terraform, and the agentic tooling that keeps it
all moving — some of which I've open-sourced (see
[Cairn](https://github.com/BigJiggity/claude-plugins)). This blog is where I
write up what I build, why I built it that way, and what broke along the road.
